# 📊 Student Performance Analysis

A comprehensive Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) project completed as part of the **Pluto Academy Data Analytics Internship**. This project analyzes student performance data to identify key factors affecting academic achievement, detect at-risk students, and provide actionable recommendations to improve educational outcomes.

---

# Problem Statement

A school principal wants to understand the factors affecting student performance in examinations. The goal is to identify the key factors influencing academic scores, detect at-risk students, and provide recommendations that can improve academic performance in the next academic year.

---

# Objectives

- Explore and understand the dataset.
- Perform data exploration and cleaning.
- Analyze factors affecting student performance.
- Create meaningful visualizations.
- Identify at-risk students.
- Prepare a Principal's Report with recommendations.

---

# Dataset


- **Dataset:** Students Performance in Exams
- **Source:** Kaggle
- **Records:** 1000 Students
- **Features:** Demographic information, parental education, lunch type, test preparation course, mathematics score, reading score, and writing score.

Dataset Link:
https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/spscientist/students-performance-in-exams

# KPI Summary

The following Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) were calculated during the analysis:

- Total Students
- Average Mathematics Score
- Average Reading Score
- Average Writing Score
- Highest Total Score
- Lowest Total Score
- Number of At-Risk Students

# Visualizations

The notebook includes the following visualizations:
- Box Plot – Average Score by Parental Education
- Bar Chart – Average Score by Test Preparation Course
- Correlation Heatmap – Mathematics, Reading and Writing Scores
- Grouped Bar Chart – Gender vs Subject Performance
- Histogram – Distribution of Total Scores
- Scatter Plot – Reading Score vs Mathematics Score

---

# Key Findings

- Students whose parents have higher education levels generally perform better academically.
- Students completing the test preparation course achieve higher average scores.
- Mathematics, Reading, and Writing scores show strong positive correlations.
- Female students generally perform better in Reading and Writing, while male students perform slightly better in Mathematics.
- A group of At-Risk students requires targeted academic intervention.

---

# Recommendations

- Increase participation in test preparation programs.
- Provide academic support for At-Risk students.
- Encourage greater parental involvement in student learning.
